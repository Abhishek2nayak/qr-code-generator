India processes over 14 billion UPI transactions per month. What is a UPI QR Code?

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) is India's real-time payment system operated by NPCI. A UPI QR code encodes your UPI ID (also called a Virtual Payment Address, or VPA) into a scannable pattern. When a customer scans it with GPay, PhonePe, Paytm, Amazon Pay, BHIM, or any UPI app, the payment screen opens pre-filled with your details — they just enter the amount (or confirm it if you've pre-set one) and tap Pay.

The UPI QR standard is the same across all apps because it follows the BHIM UPI protocol — so one QR code works for all platforms. You don't need a separate QR code for each app.

What is a UPI ID?

Your UPI ID is your payment address — it looks like an email address. Examples:

  • yourname@okicici — ICICI Bank
  • 9876543210@ybl — PhonePe / Yes Bank
  • yourname@okhdfcbank — HDFC Bank
  • yourname@paytm — Paytm
  • yourname@axisbank — Axis Bank

Find your UPI ID in your GPay, PhonePe, or banking app under Profile or Payment Settings.

Fixed Amount vs Variable Amount UPI QR

There are two types of UPI QR codes:

  • Fixed amount QR — best for specific products (e.g. a ₹500 thali plate or a ₹199 haircut). The amount is pre-filled in the payer's app, reducing errors.
  • Variable amount QR — best for a general shop counter where different customers pay different amounts. The payer enters the amount themselves.

Most shopkeepers use a variable amount QR at their counter and generate fixed-amount QR codes for specific product price lists or invoices.

Is a UPI QR Code Free?

Yes. Generating the QR code is free. And unlike card payments, there are no merchant discount rate (MDR) fees on UPI transactions — NPCI mandated zero MDR for UPI since January 2020. The customer pays nothing extra either. The full amount reaches your UPI-linked bank account directly.

UPI QR Code vs UPI Link vs UPI Intent

  • UPI QR code — scannable image. Best for physical displays, print, and in-person payments.
  • UPI payment link — a clickable URL (e.g. upi://pay?pa=…). Best for sharing on WhatsApp, email, or websites.
  • UPI intent (deeplink) — opens a specific UPI app directly. Best for app-to-app payments in your own mobile app.

Tips for a Professional UPI QR Code Display

  • Print at A5 or larger and laminate for counter display.
  • Add your shop name or logo to the printout (not the QR code itself).
  • Include your UPI ID in text below the QR so customers can type it manually if scanning fails.
  • Test monthly — scan the code yourself to verify it still works.
  • For outdoor use, choose a matte laminate to reduce glare and improve scannability.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is it safe to share my UPI QR code publicly?

Yes — your UPI QR code only allows people to send money to you. It cannot be used to debit from your account. However, do not scan unknown UPI QR codes you receive from strangers, as scammers sometimes send "collect request" QRs disguised as payment QRs.

Can I create a UPI QR code without a merchant account?

Yes. Any personal UPI ID works. You don't need a business account or merchant registration to accept UPI payments via QR code.

How much can I receive via a UPI QR code?

The per-transaction limit is ₹1,00,000 (₹1 lakh) for most UPI apps. Daily limits vary by bank but typically range from ₹1–2 lakh per day for personal accounts.
