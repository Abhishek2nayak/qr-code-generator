WiFi QR Code Generator: Share Your Network Without Sharing the Password

May 2025 · 6 min read · By Free QR Code Generator Team

Tired of reading out a 16-character WiFi password to every guest? A WiFi QR code lets anyone connect to your network by simply pointing their phone camera at a printed code — no password required. This guide shows you exactly how to create one for free in under two minutes.

What is a WiFi QR Code?

A WiFi QR code encodes your network name (SSID), security type, and password into a standard QR format that smartphones can decode natively. When someone scans it with their iPhone (iOS 11+) or Android camera (Android 9+), the device automatically prompts them to join the network — no manual entry needed.

The format is called MECARD WiFi and looks like this internally:

WIFI:T:WPA;S:MyNetworkName;P:MyPassword;;

This is all handled automatically by our generator — you just fill in the form fields.

Where to Use Your WiFi QR Code

  • Home — Frame it near your router or at your front door for visiting friends and family.
  • Café or restaurant — Place it on tables, at the till, or on the takeaway packaging.
  • Hotel or Airbnb — Include it in the welcome booklet or on a laminated card in the room.
  • Office — Put it in the meeting room, reception area, or visitors' lounge.
  • Events — Add it to event programmes, badge lanyards, or signage.
  • Co-working space — Display it on every desk pod and phone booth.

Is It Safe to Use a WiFi QR Code?

A WiFi QR code does encode your password inside the pattern — anyone who scans the code can view the raw data. This means:

  • The QR image itself should be treated as sensitive. Don't post it publicly online.
  • Anyone who photographs the QR code gains network access. Display it in controlled areas only.
  • For guest networks: create a separate guest SSID with limited bandwidth and access. Encode that password instead of your main network's password.
  • Your password is never sent to any server when using our generator — everything is processed in your browser.

Does a WiFi QR Code Work on All Phones?

Yes, with one caveat:

  • iPhone (iOS 11+): Open the Camera app, point at the QR code, tap the notification banner.
  • Android (Android 9+ / Pixel): Open the Camera app, point, tap the popup.
  • Samsung (One UI 2.0+): Use the Camera app or the QR scanner in the notification shade.
  • Older Android (pre-9): Download a QR code reader app like "QR Code Reader" from the Play Store.

What Happens If I Change My WiFi Password?

Since our QR codes are static (data is baked into the pattern), you'll need to generate a new QR code with the updated password and reprint it. This is why we recommend using a separate, simpler guest password that you change less frequently. If you need a QR code that can be updated without reprinting, you'd need a dynamic QR code service (which typically requires a paid subscription).
